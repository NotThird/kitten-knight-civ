Policy: task quotas (AI plan bias)
These multipliers bias the colony-wide worker plan. 0 = never (unless safety/emergency), 1 = default, 2 = strongly prefer. Great for nudging specialization.
Role quotas (gentle specialization targets)
Optional. If you set a quota above 0, the AI will try to keep that many kittens in the role (still overridden by safety rules/emergencies).

Kitten Council (AI policy suggestions) NEW
Occasional bottom-up nudges from individual kittens (based on their likes/traits + colony status). Accepting adjusts policy multipliers slightly.
Factions (values blocs)
A civ-sim layer: kittens group into simple values blocs (Food/Safety/Progress/Social). Negotiating with the biggest bloc can reduce dissent, but may pull policy away from your ideal plan.
Bloc health (who is unhappy?)
Quick readout by values bloc: avg policy-fit, mood, and grievance. Use this to spot which bloc is driving dissent and whether a negotiation would actually help.
